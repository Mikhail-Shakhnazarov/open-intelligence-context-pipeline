## THE CONVENTION // 01

This protocol distinguishes two epistemic states using a minimal typographic convention:

- Roman text: binding within the artifact's declared authority scope (ratified output for that scope).
- Italic text: commentary (assumptions, uncertainty, missing evidence, suggested improvements, non-binding proposals).

Where rendering does not support italics, a conformant representation must still preserve the distinction by an explicit marker (implementation-defined) and must not collapse the states.

## THE PROMOTION RULE // 02

Provisional content does not become binding by storage or repetition. Promotion from italic to roman requires explicit resolution:

- an operator decision,
- a test outcome,
- or imported evidence.

Update proposals are provisional by definition. Storage of proposals is not promotion.

## DERIVATION MARKINGS // 03

Claims may be tagged with derivation markings:

- Stated: extracted directly from an identified source record.
- Inferred: derived through a declared rule or reasoning step.
- Assumed: adopted without direct evidence for operational convenience.

Derivation markings make provenance tractable without requiring a full citation per sentence. Durable registries should require derivation markings for new entries.

## FAILURE MODES // 04

Layer collapse: italics are used as decoration, not epistemic status.

Promotion by inertia: proposals harden into long-term state because they are stored, not because they were approved.

Missing derivation markings: durable claims appear without provenance cues; later review cannot determine reliability.
