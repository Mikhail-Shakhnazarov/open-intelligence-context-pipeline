# Workplace use and boundaries

This note belongs to the team path. Start at `docs/team-quickstart.md` for the minimal end-to-end workflow.

Work material is often not "personal" but still must be treated as **private**: confidential projects, internal numbers, client data, credentials, regulated material, or any content covered by policy or contract.

This protocol uses "private" as a custody category: material that must remain operator-/organization-controlled and is prohibited from external emission by default.

## Recommended boundary posture

- Treat workplace registries as **private** and **internal** by default.
- Only create "external" packets when the included material is explicitly eligible for emission (sanitized, approved, or non-sensitive).
- Do not mix work and personal durable registries unless a gate exists that prevents cross-contamination.

## Common corporate misconception

"Enterprise accounts" reduce some risk but do not create:

- selection logs for selection decisions,
- prevention of promotion-by-inertia,
- a portable continuity layer across tools and policy changes.

Governance is a workflow property, not a vendor property.

## Not legal advice

This repository provides a workflow protocol, not legal or compliance advice. Organizational counsel and policy owners determine what is allowed to leave the boundary.
